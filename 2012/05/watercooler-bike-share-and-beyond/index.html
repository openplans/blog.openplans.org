Watercooler: Bike Share and Beyond

Posted on May 8, 2012 by Anthony Denaro
Here’s some of the chatter that bubbled up around our office water cooler over the last few weeks:
+ “Unfortunately for car companies,” Jordan Weissmann noted at theatlantic.com a couple weeks back, “today’s teens and twenty-somethings don’t seem all that interested in buying a set of wheels. They’re not even particularly keen on driving.” But why?….
Look familiar? Philly’s Bike Share map is based on Shareabouts

…O, here’s why – people are getting viable transportation options!  Over in Philadelphia, the recently announced Bike Share Philadelphia is using OpenPlans’ Shareabouts as it’s initial public outreach tool.  If you’re in Philly, click over to suggest a location for a bike share station….

…While in New York City, with a 10 months jump on the process, prepares for the arrival of it’s public bike-sharing program in July. With all the crowdsourced station data collected, DOT has been working with Community Boards on where and how the stations will be placed throughout the city….  via NYTimes.com

…while Streetsblog NY broke just how much (or little, really) NYC Bike Share is going to cost to be a member of.

+ In San Francisco, ImproveSF will enable the city and county of San Francisco to leverage the expertise of  the city’s braintrust to solve the city’s most challenging problems.

+ Somewhat similarly the City of New York prepares for it’s first green hackathon.  It will give the opportunity for data scientists, developers and designers to use environmental data to create tools and apps that empower New Yorkers to live greener, greater lives.  Sign up here

+ As for apps that are already hacked together Triptrop NYC is useful for door to door travel time prediction  seeing how long it would take to walk to an apartment or travel time to work etc).

+Google Hotel Finder, currently in beta, works in a similar matter, allowing you to search within an area you designate.

+ Mappiness  is a mobile app whose aim is to collect data on happiness across the UK – It’ll beep you once (or more) a day to ask how you’re feeling, and a few circumstantial things: who you’re with, where you are, what you’re doing (if you’re outdoors, you can also take a photo).  Our question is: Can we really quantify happiness?

+ Neighborland allows you to share your ideas and insights for your city, support your neighbors’ ideas, and connect with people who share your interests. No issue is too big or too small to bring up on Neighborland.

+ Mayor Michael R. Bloomberg today announced that Rahul N. Merchant will serve as New York City’s first Citywide Chief Information and Innovation Officer and as Commissioner of the Department of Information, Technology and Telecommunications.  We’re glad to have you – welcome aboard Rahul!

+ And to end on a really nice note – Philly Streets Kudos – Philly’s Streets Dept dialing compliments from their constituents back into the community.  It’s nice to here something nice.
