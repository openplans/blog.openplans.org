Watercooler: Up, down, all around.

Posted on March 8, 2013 by Anthony Denaro

The last two weeks have provided simulated views of real cities, simulated views of simulated cities, and new views on old words and old designs.  Here’s some of the things we were chatting about in the office this week.

+ “SimCity for real” – Dr Steven E. Koonin at New York University’s Center for Urban Science and Progress is building the visualization and modeling tools to allow for simulation of real cities pulling in real data to predict future behaviors. End game: to make cities work better.  via NY Times

+ The OpenPlans team competed in an actual SimCity tournament.  via co.exist

+ While Koonin is predicting the future, Chicago’s Chief Information Officer Brett Goldstein is proposing the SmartChicago Analytics Platform. It’s one of 20 ideas vying for Bloomberg’s Mayor Challenge. It would be an open source platform to compile data from 311 systems, crime statistics, and historical data to better anticipate events that require city resources, like emergencies or seasonal peak usage.  via mashable

+ David Eaves talks about the increasingly pervasive idea of an open source suite of services for municipal governments.  via David Eaves 

+ Concerned the conditions on your street now? Here’s a tool for Chicago residents to find out when the street sweeper will hit your block is getting swept.

+ Abandoned properties today, beautiful neighborhoods tomorrow? This map from Louisville, Kentucky is the first step towards it. This   via Metropolitan Housing Coalition

+ Cool map from our friends at ITO World showing the implications of London’s 10 years congestion pricing.  via Streetsblog 

+ Brookyn’s Barclay Center’s gee-whiz parking solution. Check out the reason the Dean St bike lane is usually blocked by an idling tractor-trailer. via NY Post

+ Precinct 5 vs Martinique.  This map compares each of  NYC’s police precinct’s homicide rate with countries with a similar rate.  Our home precinct pairs up with beautiful sunny Martinique.  via The New York World

+ Binder full of designs? New York City Transit’s 1970’s v1 Graphics Standards Manual was found, scanned and posted. One enthusiastic design has described its uncovering as exciting as discovering the “sword in the stone of transit wayfinding manuals”.  Nerd, please, stifle yourself.  via fawny.org 

+ Is it time to give up the term “Mobile”?  The idea of the mobile website as unique from the desktop web is through, argues Jim Ramsden. It’s time to think about sites as device-universal. via Jim Ramsden

+ A set from Aliens 2. Nope! Its construction update shots of East Side Access.  This data comes in photo form, enjoy. via MTA
