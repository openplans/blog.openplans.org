OpenBag: Vizzuality

Posted on November 4, 2011 by Anthony Denaro

Vizzuality, the Madrid-based geo-spatial mapping company, has been co-habitating in OpenPlans’ office since June and we’ve had the chance to get to know them well. Javier de la Torres, Lead Developer and Co-founder stepped up at OpenBag this week.  While his face is familiar around these parts, Vizzuality work wasn’t; like finding out that your best friend saved a baby from a burning building, Javier wowed us with what Vizzuality has accomplished.

Vizzuality explores preservation, diversity, environmental and ecological issues – to help foster engagement and participation.  Their projects beautifully visualize data collected by institutions and research teams. They’re like OpenPlans, but out to make the wild a beter place: more bark than steel, more feathers than neon bike vests, more forests and jungles than cities and towns.

For example  the Antartic Field Guides help scientist and amateurs in their preparation for a trip to the Antarctica. With AFG they can create a custom field guide with all the species they are interested in.  Their Old Weather interface uses data pulled from the UK Royal Navy’s ship logs from the early 1900’s(!) from which volunteers transcribe the weather information.  That is then used to visualize day to day weather from the past. This data is being used to develop  better climate models.  It’s a mindblowing exercise.  And a really successful one.  Check out CartoDB, an open source platform that quickly facilitates the mapping of data and creation of location based apps and websites, easily and affordably.  Check out their other projects here.

Who speaks at OpenBag? Anyone who is engaged in transportation, programming, mapping, diagramming, public space and urban issues and is taking the creative steps to push their respective field forward.  Or just anyone who is doing something we think is interesting – being that we’re all nerds here, it tends to be tech, planning or transit related.

Have something interesting you want to share?  Drop us an inquiry.
